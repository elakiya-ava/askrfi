Code of Conduct
1. Does your company have a formal Ethics and Compliance program in place?

Source: CRD 1.0 Feb '25
Subject owner(s): Jenny Stokes
1. Does your company have a written Code of Conduct / Ethics? If so, Please provide a corporate statement on it. [CHECK JENNY'S LATEST TEXT]
Yes.
Avalere Health is committed to operating at high ethical standards, in full compliance with all laws, rules and regulations governing our activities in the UK, USA, and other jurisdictions in which we operate. Consequently, every employee has a responsibility to respond with honesty and integrity in every situation, and to follow high standards of moral and ethical conduct in all we do. The Group is also subject to operating within the ethics and compliance requirements of our clients as mandated through our Master Service Agreements and other contractual obligations. All employees are required to attest to the code of conduct on an annual basis–this covers all core aspects of business integrity to which we hold ourselves.
Source: CRD 1.0 Feb '25
Subject owner(s): Jenny Stokes
2. Do you have established mechanisms and practices for reporting and investigating ethical misconduct and for disciplining employees for misconduct?
Yes.

Subject owner(s): Jenny Stokes
Compliance training
1. Do you have a formal, documented training program?
We have a formal Group-led Compliance training program whereby colleagues are required to complete online modules on an annual basis with respect to key risk areas. Example of modules include Anti-Bribery & Corruption, Data Protection and Modern Slavery.
Source: Recent RFI Response Oct '25.
Subject owner(s): Jenny Stokes
2. Does your training program include training on (local) regulations?
The Group-led Compliance training focuses on managing key legal and regulatory areas. Relevant legislation includes: Bribery Act 2010, US Foreign Corrupt Practices Act 1977, United States’ Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996, General Data Protection Regulation, GDPR (EU 2016/679), the UK GDPR and Data Protection Act, UK Modern Slavery Act 2015, Criminal Finances Act 2017 (the “Act”), Public Interest Disclosure Act (PIDA) 1998 and EU Whistleblowing Directive 2019.
Source: Recent RFI Responses Oct '25.
Subject owner(s): Jenny Stokes
