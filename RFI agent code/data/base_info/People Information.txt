3.  Do you have a formal process/policies or recognised practices that makes clear that decisions around recruitment, development and promotion are made on the basis of ability, merit and performance?
Yes. Our dedicated in-house Talent team are highly experienced across the TA/recruiting spectrum and are experts when it comes to managing recruitment processes for urgent client requirements, across multiple accounts, areas of expertise, and locations. Recruiting processes vary across capabilities and teams, however we have developed robust and inclusive interviewing processes that are based on Avalere Health values and the required roles skills and competencies (this includes scoring matrixes, interview tasks e.g, presentations, writing, editorial tasks etc). The majority of roles across Avalere Health involve initial telephone screening interviews, skills-based assessments, and at least 2 interviews which are conducted by interviewers who have completed D&I, unconscious bias, and interview training. All employees have background checks completed by a trusted external partner, subject to their role and team. Recruiting methodology varies depending on team/client requirements, however it is based on the 'attract, engage, recruit, retain' model. Our recruiting activities are focused on building strategic relationships with stakeholders and candidates, whilst developing organizational initiatives that drive candidate engagement to build long-term, diverse pipelines of talent for current and future recruiting needs. Avalere Health has 2 salary/promotion review windows each year. Guidance is provided to leaders about salary increases and promotions needing to be made on the basis of ability, merit and performance, and we set eligibility criteria to help leaders do this in a fair and consistent manner.
Source: Recent RFI Responses Oct '25
Subject owner(s): Kirsty Wear

5.
Please describe your policy relating to employee pay and wage laws
It’s vital to us that we ensuring compliance with the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) and other applicable federal, state, and local regulations in relation to pay and benefits. To do this we set salary benchmarks that meet or exceed the minimum wage, paying eligible employees overtime at a rate of one and a half times their regular pay for hours worked beyond 40 in a workweek, and keeping accurate records of hours worked through timesheets. We conduct regular reviews of our salary benchmarks and have two opportunities a year to adjust employees’ wages. By doing this we create a fair, legally compliant work environment that respects the rights of all employees.
Source: Recent RFI response Oct '25
